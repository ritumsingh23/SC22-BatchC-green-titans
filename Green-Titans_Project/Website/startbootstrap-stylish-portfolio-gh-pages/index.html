The Big Why

We are attempting to solve the problem of identifying counterfeit banknotes, the currency that we are trying to identify would be the swiss banknote. About 1 in 100,000 banknotes in Switzerland is counterfeit and about 1,000 counterfeit banknotes are confiscated each year in order to make this process of catching, identifying and thwarting the use of counterfeit bills. It won't be possible to catch each person each time they print a counterfeit bill. Instead it should be caught when the bill is about to be used. This means that the business or person that is receiving this bill should be able to verify that the bill is “real” or counterfeit. This means that they should be equipped with the skills and tools to do this. By using the growing popularity of the technology of AI, with our project anyone with access to the internet will be able to verify the originality of the bill being used. This can contribute a skill set the people have that have no knowledge on these aspects, and are vulnerable to fall victim to being scammed by these counterfeit bills, by allowing them to identify and restrict further use of counterfeit bills in the nation.

Top X Bottom

The main change analyzed in the Bottom x Top plot is that bottom measurements for counterfeit bills tend to be much larger than real bills, which fall under 10 mm, while real bills hovering around 9.5 mm.

Length x Diagonal

The main change analyzed in the Diagonal x Length plot is that the diagonal measurements of counterfeit bills tend to be a lot shorter than real bills, which tend to be at least 141 mm, with counterfeit bills being 140 or less.

Bottom x Top x Diagonal

The 2 main changes analyzed in the Diagonal x Bottom x Top plot are the differences in diagonal measurements and top measurements. In real bills, the diagonal measurements tend to be much larger and the top measurements tend to be a lot smaller, and vice versa for the counterfeit bills. There is also a difference for bottom measurements, which also tend to be smaller in real bills and larger in counterfeit ones.

Left x Right x Diagonal

The main changes analyzed in the Diagonal x Left x Right plot are the differences in diagonal measurements and right measurements. The real banknotes show a smaller right size than the counterfeit bills, with barely any plots going bigger than 130 mm. On the contrary, the diagonal measurements are much larger for the real bills and a lot smaller for the counterfeit bills.

Diagonal x Bottom

In this graph we decided to show the dimensions of the bottom and the diagonal of the banknote with the yellow being the Diagonal and bottom length of the counterfeit bank notes. As you can see on the graph the counterfeit diagonal lengths are smaller than the legit banknotes.

Right x Left x Bottom

In this data set we compared the lengths of the bottom, left, and right of the counterfeit and legit banknotes. As you can see from the graph the length of most of the bottom banknotes are much bigger than the legit ones. Most of the counterfeit bills have right measurements fairly larger than the real bills, which are smaller other than a few outliers.

Banknotes Machine Learning Model

So for this project, when we used our machine learning models, it was very important to us that we achieve high accuracy, and with the amount of machine learning models we were using, we wanted perfection. And perfection we got. KNN, Randomforest, SVC, Logistic Regression, and Boosting all gave us a 100% accuracy score, which we were extremely proud of. This showed our machine was extremely clean and organized, that too before hyperparameter tuning, which we were proud of. However, we quickly learnt that our data had missed out in one place. Decision tree. Decision tree had not given us a 100% accuracy, only 97.5. While this isn't a devastating loss due to it only being a difference of 2.5 points, it was a curiosity peaking concept, for me at least. Regardless, our machine learning training and testing showed great success, therefore that's a job well done.
